“ Everything turns to waste eventually. ”

Rot is produced when Food items (excluding Eggs) are left alone and spoil after a certain time. Rot can be used as Fertilizer for Farming (making crops on Basic Farms and Improved Farms grow faster) and reviving Grass Tufts and Berry Bushes. It will fertilize 1/4 of the amount Manure would and advance growth 1/5 as much as Guano would.

Mobs, such as Pigs or Smallish Tallbirds will eat Rot left on the ground, but they can't be given rot directly. Since Rot doesn't restore any Health or Hunger to Mobs, they will consume all Rot left on the ground.

Rot may appear along with items such as Rope and Bird Traps in a Set Piece trap, near a Chest and/or Backpack. Opening either container will cause the player's perishable items to turn to Rot, though this can be avoided by dropping the items a distance away first.

Tips

  • A quick way to obtain Rot is to leave Roasted Berries or Fish lying on the ground (preferably in a walled in area, so that animals can't eat them).
  • WX-78 can eat spoiled food, but it will still be damaged if it eats Rot.
  • A Tallbird nest near a Rabbit Hole or another area where weak mobs spawn is a good sources of Rot, as long as the player can avoid getting hit and they stay close enough to allow for the hole and nest to load.
  • The food with the lowest rot time is Lichen, which is found in Ruins with a 2 day spoil time. Its opposite is the Powdercake, which takes 18750 days to turn to Rot.
  • If the player has a lot of Rot, it can be saved for fueling fires.

Trivia

  • Rot originally looked like a blue ball. Though the texture is now a misshapen blob, multiple characters still refer to it as a "ball".
