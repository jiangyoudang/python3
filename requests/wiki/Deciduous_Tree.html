The Birchnut Tree is a tree found in the Reign of Giants DLC. When chopped, it'll drop 1-3 Logs and 1 Birchnut (2 during Autumn). Standing under one offers moderate protection from Rain and Overheating.

They are generated randomly with each new map and are found mainly in Grasslands and Deciduous Forests.

    Angering the Trees

    When cutting down a fully sized Birchnut Tree that drops Birchnuts from day 3 and after, there is a small chance that other Birchnut Trees in the area will "awaken" and begin attacking anything in the area, unless it is Winter. The chance increases with the number of days survived from 3% on day 3 to 33% on day 70. Unlike the somewhat similar Treeguards, angry Birchnut Trees have to be felled with an Axe (12 swings) or any of the other methods that will destroy the average tree. Torching an angry Birchnut Tree spawns another, final wave of Birchnutters. After enough time has passed, angered Birchnut Trees will calm down and return to their normal state. The player can also plant Birchnuts nearby to attempt to pacify them. Once an angry Birchnut Tree has been killed, its stump can be dug up with a Shovel to yield an additional Living Log. An angry Birchnut Tree spawns up to 5 Birchnutters at a time. They will also do a root attack that homes in slightly on the players position.

    Seasonal Changes

    During Autumn, the tree's leaves will turn a dying brown and slowly fall. Full-sized birchnut trees yield an extra Birchnut when cut down during Autumn.

    During Winter, Birchnut Trees will be in a state of hibernation and as such will never attack the player. They will not, however, drop any Birchnuts when chopped down.

    Tips

    • Try thinking of the Birchnut trees as a source of Living Logs and Nightmare Fuel more than a source of regular logs, due to the mass of Poison Birchnut Trees that spawn from a minimal amount of chopping.
    • Keeping one Poison Birchnut Tree alive will result in a useful 'farm' of sorts for Birchnuts, which can be used for food, but more importantly more Living Logs and Nightmare Fuel.
    • Take care while chopping these, amassing birchnuts will summon a Dragonfly during Summer and may be a challenge to fight it.

    Trivia

    • In the first version of the Reign of Giants, the Birchnut Tree was simply named "Deciduous Tree".
    • Chopping a Poison Birchnut Tree may lead to others waking, which can end in a whole forest being chopped down.
    • Though not true Treeguards, the similarities between the Poison Birchnut Trees and the Treeguards has led to people referring to them as 'Birchnut Treeguards' and 'Birchguards'.
    • Despite having white bark, the tree drops normal logs with brown bark.
    • Pigs and other mobs that are able to smash structures are the only mobs that can destroy a Poison Birchnut Tree.
    • Wilson's quote upon examination of a Birchnut Tree is a reference to what he says when he examines an Evergreen Tree, "It's all piney".

    Bugs

    • If the player is too far away from the Birchnut Tree when it turns passive again, it will continue to appear with the poison build and drop the poison build's loot, though it will no longer fight the player.
    • If the player survives a night near the limits of the tree's attack range, there is a slight possibility that the tree will be pacified the next day.
