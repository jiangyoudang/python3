A Contested Area is any area in the map which has two or more natural denizens that fight each other regularly in a perpetual war. These areas are useful to the player as it already has many dead creatures from previous fights and allows the player a much easier time of acquiring loot from otherwise too dangerous of areas.

Types of foes to contest areas

Spider (most likely contestant, aggressive)

Tentacle (aggressive in a small area)

Pig (defensive in most cases, consumes meat)

Beefalo (aggressive during mating season, defensive when not mating)

Bee (easily killed, defensive only)

Merm (aggressive when near houses)

Tallbird (aggressive in a large area, goes to sleep at night)

Frogs (aggressive in small area)

Spider Dens (and their foes)

Tentacles- Since tentacles attack anything that comes in range, and are quite powerful, a group of spider dens in the swamp will constantly have a hapless spider walk over, get attacked, and entice the whole den to attack. As long as the attack is not overwhelming, this situation can continue for quite a while. It is possible for the spiders to kill the tentacle, and end the war.

Pig Houses - When pigs wander onto spiderwebs near dens, they instantly aggro nearby spiders, causing the pigs to aggro as well. This battle continues forever as pigs do not kill spider dens and spiders do not destroy the pig houses where additional pigs spawn.

Rabbit Hutches - Rabbit Hutches are similar to pig houses, but Bunnymen do not eat meat and come out at the same time as spiders, and so are subsequently more effective. It has been recommended to have a ratio of 14 rabbit hutches to 3 level 3 tier spider dens. The drawback is bunny hutches require rarer and more valuable items to craft. It requires 10 carrots, 4 bunny fluff and 3 boards for a bunny hutch and 3 cut stone, 4 boards and 4 pig skin for a pig house

Beefalo Herds (mating)- This is probably the weakest kind of contested area and usually temporary. It almost always ends in the spiders favor. This area is only contested when Beefalos are in mating season, further diminishing the chance of a battle starting.


It should be important to note that spider dens in particular have the ability to grow and become stronger, eventually enveloping them, turning a contested area into a one-sided slaughter.
