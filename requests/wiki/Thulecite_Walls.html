Walls are craftable Items and deployable Structures that prevent the player's and Mobs' movement. Hay, Wood, Stone, and Thulecite walls have 100, 200, 400, and 800 maximum health, respectively. Contrary to popular belief, walls seem to not be able to restrict movement when placed next to water.

Hay Wall

The Hay Wall is the weakest of the four Walls, a craftable Item and a deployable Structure that prevents the player's and Mobs' movement. Once built, it can be repaired or upgraded into a second tier with another piece of Hay Wall or enough Cut Grass. It can be attacked and destroyed, leaving a destroyed Hay Wall. Hammering the wall will yield 2 Cut Grass from second tier and 1 Cut Grass from other tiers. It has a maximum of 100 health and 50 health when first built before upgrades. The Hay Wall is highly flammable and one should exercise caution when fighting Red Hounds near it. If burned they will drop 1 ash.

Wood Wall

The Wood Wall is one of four Walls, a craftable Item and Structure that prevents the player's and Mobs' movement. Once built, it can be repaired or upgraded into a second tier with another piece of Wood Wall or enough Twigs, Logs, or Boards. It can be attacked and destroyed, leaving a destroyed Wood Wall. It is also highly flammable, so measures should be taken when fighting Red Hounds. Using a Hammer on the wall will yield 1 Log from tiers one and two and 2 Logs from tier 3. It has a maximum of 200 health, and begins with 100 health when first built before being upgraded.

Stone Wall

The Stone Wall is the second toughest of the 4 walls, behind the Thulecite Wall. It is crafted as an Item and deployed as a Structure that prevents movement by both creatures and characters. A Stone Wall is first built in its second tier, but can be repaired (upgraded) into a third or fourth tier by adding another piece of Stone Wall or enough Rocks or Cut Stone. It can be attacked to reduce it to first tier and then destroyed, leaving a destroyed Stone Wall. Hammering the wall will yield 2 Rocks from fourth tier and 1 Rocks from other tiers. It has 200 health when first built and a maximum of 400 health. In repairs, 1 rock will add considerably less health to the wall than 1 stone wall will.  Since it ultimately takes 6 rocks to make 6 stone walls, using rocks directly to repair a wall is very inefficient.  Likewise using cut stone is a loss versus using another piece of stone wall.  In addition, the cost to fully repair a completely destroyed wall is much higher than just breaking the wall down with a hammer, and starting fresh.  Once they are broken down completely it can take 8 or 9 pieces of wall to completely build back up to a top level wall, due to the way the repair function works. Always hammer it down and start fresh.

Thulecite Wall

Thulecite walls are the toughest of the walls, having 400 health when initially placed and a maximum health of 800 when fully upgraded.  Thulecite Walls are craftable using an Ancient Pseudoscience Station and cost 1 Thulecite. When broken down with a Hammer they yield Thulecite Fragments.

They can be placed down to block the path of both creatures and the player. When built and placed it will be at its 3rd tier of repair. It can be upgraded into 2 more tiers with Thulecite Fragments or Thulecite. When hammered the 5th tier will yield 2 Thulecite fragments, while the other tiers will only give one. This means that when you don't repair a 3rd tier wall and it gets damaged it's better to hammer it down and replace it rather than repair it.

They are also found naturally in the Ruins, sometimes around Ancient Guardians and Damaged Clockworks.

Trivia

  • Before they were implemented Thulecite Walls had a greyish color instead of the current golden one.
  • Since Thulecite Walls only take one Thulecite to build and each wall drops Thulecite Fragments when hammered, the player can make the walls, hammer them down when they get useless and still craft the original Thulecite back as long as they place the walls seperatly and don't combine any of them.
  • The first three kinds of walls could be a reference to "The Three Little Pigs". The pigs in the story each had a house made of either straw (hay wall), sticks (wood wall), or bricks (stone wall). The presence of Hounds being an enemy where walls are important to have could also be a reference to The Three Little Pigs.
  • Prior to the Moderately Friendly Update, walls could be repaired using healing items, such as the Honey Poultice.
  • Maxwell breaks the fourth wall when he examines walls in the inventory. (Quote: "Pocket-sized wall pieces. Yup.")
