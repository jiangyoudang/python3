The Varg, also known as the Warg, is an enemy in "Reign of Giants" DLC. When tracking a Koalefant, there is a chance for the Varg to be tracked down instead. This chance starts at 5% on day one, and gradually increases to 33% chance on day 100. The player is unable to tell which animal will be at the end of the tracks before encountering it. When killed, he drops around 4-6 Monster Meat, and 1-3 Hound's Tooth.

Attack Pattern

The Varg will howl, calling forth two normal Hounds to the battle. In close range, it attacks by biting, with a two hit delay. It moves slowly, but attacks fast and has a large range for its jaws. Vargs are also able to call forth special Hounds (at least 1) to join the fight (Blue hounds and Red Hounds, depending on the season).

Uses

The Varg can effectively be used as a way to spawn hounds if there are no nearby Hound Mounds (or all the Hound Mounds in the current world are destroyed). One way to do this is leading the Varg into a pen with a gap on the wall. Run into the pen and put the Varg to sleep once it follows inside. Exit the pen and close the gap with walls. Walk away from the sleeping Varg a fair distance and then hit it with a ranged weapon, like a Boomerang. This way it'll wake up and aggro on the player instead of on the walls. As long as the player doesn't go too far away, the Varg will keep running against the wall, not attacking it, and spawning Hounds periodically.

Trivia

  • The Warg is a wolf-like creature from Norse mythology. The term was used to refer to Fenrir and his offspring.
  • In the time since the Norsemen, "Varg" has come to mean "wolf" in Swedish.
  • J. R. R. Tolkien popularized wargs in his fantasy novels set in Middle Earth, like "The Hobbit" and "The Lord Of The Rings". They are also popular in Dungeon and Dragons where they are known as a "worg" or "Dire Wolf".
  • The Varg will always howl to summon Hounds when it can do so. It will even resurrect for a brief moment after death to do it.
